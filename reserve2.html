In The Beginning

In an attempt to find a lasting solution to a national book Crisis, a concerned writer, Chukwuemeka Ike convened a meeting of writers. publishers, and representatives of various professional groups in the book industry at Ibadan on May 24, 1990. The meeting resolved to establish the Nigerian Book Development Forum (NBDF). The forum elected Professor Ike as convener and appointed a task force with the following membership to act on its behalf when necessary. *Professor Chukwuemeka Ike, *The Hon. Dr. T.Akinola Aguda, *Mr. B.O. Bolodeoku, *Mr. E. AkinThomas, *Mrs. Mabel D. Segun, *Chief Nuhu Bature, *Dr. Bode Sowande, *Engr. Victor U. Nwankwo, *Professor Theo Vincent, *Mr Tope Popoola. *University Press Plc Ibadan voluntarily served as the secretariat for the forum and made available the part-time services of Mrs. L.L. Onifade as secretary. The task force held nine meetings and was granted four hearings by the Presidential Advisory Committee between June 1990 June 1991. After weighing the pros and cons of establishing a central book development agency as a government parastatal, the Task Force opted for a non- governmental, non-profit Foundation. On July 16, 1991, the Nigerian Book Development Forum met at Ibadan and endorsed the establishment of the Nigerian Book Foundation (NBF), thereby dissolving itself. A Board of Directors established on the same date ceased to exist in December 1992 on technical grounds. A Board of Trustees, which replaced it held its first meeting in Lagos on April 15, 1993. The Foundation set up its Secretariat at Awka, Anambra State, in October 1993, and in December 14, 1994, the National Advisory Council of the The foundation held its first meeting in Lagos.
